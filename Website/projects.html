Projects & Legal-Tech Work

A collection of ongoing and conceptual projects reflecting my interest in Technology Law, Artificial Intelligence, and Intellectual Property Rights. These projects demonstrate my ability to analyze, design, and propose legal solutions in emerging technological domains.

AI Compliance Assistant (Conceptual Legal-Tech Tool)

This project focuses on designing a legal-tech platform that assists businesses in ensuring compliance with emerging AI regulations. The tool is conceptualized to analyze AI systems, identify potential legal risks, and provide compliance recommendations based on global regulatory standards.

Key Features:

  • AI system risk classification based on regulatory frameworks
  • Automated compliance checklist generation
  • Integration with data protection and privacy guidelines

My Role:

Developing the legal framework, identifying regulatory gaps, and mapping AI risks to applicable laws such as data protection, liability, and intellectual property.

Gaming Law Regulatory Framework (India-Focused Study)

This project explores the fragmented regulatory landscape of online gaming in India. It aims to create a unified legal framework addressing issues such as taxation, skill vs. chance classification, and consumer protection.

Key Focus Areas:

  • Legal classification of online games
  • Analysis of GST implications on gaming platforms
  • Comparison with international gaming regulations

Outcome:

A proposed centralized regulatory model to reduce inconsistencies and improve legal clarity for gaming companies operating in India.

Intellectual Property Protection for AI-Generated Content

This project analyzes ownership and protection challenges surrounding AI-generated content such as text, art, and software. It examines whether current copyright laws are sufficient to address AI-driven creativity.

Key Issues Explored:

  • Ownership of AI-generated works
  • Authorship and originality standards
  • Liability in case of infringement

My Contribution:

Developing legal arguments and proposing reforms to ensure protection of both developers and users of AI systems.

Data Privacy Risk Assessment Model

This project involves creating a structured model to assess privacy risks for organizations handling personal data. The model is aligned with GDPR principles and Indian data protection laws.

Features:

  • Data classification and sensitivity mapping
  • Risk scoring system for compliance evaluation
  • Recommendations for mitigation strategies

Application:

Useful for startups and businesses to understand and improve their data protection compliance before regulatory scrutiny.

Legal Research Automation Concept

A conceptual tool aimed at simplifying legal research using AI-based document analysis and case law summarization. The system would assist lawyers in quickly identifying relevant precedents and extracting key insights.

Proposed Capabilities:

  • Automated case law summarization
  • Keyword-based intelligent search
  • Prediction of legal outcomes based on past judgments

Objective:

To enhance efficiency in legal research and reduce time spent on manual analysis.
