Research Papers & Legal Drafts

This section represents my engagement with contemporary legal issues at the intersection of law, technology, and policy. The following research works reflect my analytical approach to emerging challenges in Artificial Intelligence, Data Protection, and Intellectual Property Law.

Artificial Intelligence and the Transformation of Legal Systems

This research examines the integration of Artificial Intelligence into legal systems, focusing on automation in legal research, predictive analytics in litigation, and AI-assisted decision-making processes. The study evaluates how AI is reshaping traditional legal workflows and raising complex regulatory and ethical challenges.

Key Areas Explored:

  • Use of AI in legal research and document review
  • Predictive analytics in litigation outcomes
  • Ethical concerns including bias and accountability

Conclusion:

The research proposes a hybrid regulatory model where AI tools assist legal processes but remain subject to human oversight, ensuring fairness and accountability.

Data Privacy in India: Evolution from PDP Bill to DPDP Act

This paper analyzes India's journey from the Personal Data Protection Bill to the Digital Personal Data Protection Act. It examines how India is shaping its data governance framework in response to global privacy standards.

Focus Areas:

  • Consent-based data processing models
  • Cross-border data transfer regulations
  • Role of Data Protection Authorities

Insight:

The research highlights the need for stronger enforcement mechanisms and clearer compliance guidelines for businesses operating in the digital ecosystem.

Intellectual Property Challenges in AI-Generated Content

This study explores the legal ambiguity surrounding ownership and protection of AI-generated works. It questions whether current copyright frameworks can adequately address AI-driven creativity.

Issues Addressed:

  • Authorship and originality in AI-generated content
  • Ownership rights between developers and users
  • Infringement risks in AI outputs

Proposed Reform:

The paper suggests introducing a new category of intellectual property protection specifically designed for AI-generated works.

Regulation of Online Gaming in India: Need for a Unified Framework

This research analyzes the fragmented legal landscape governing online gaming in India. It explores inconsistencies across states and the challenges posed by overlapping laws.

Core Analysis:

  • Distinction between games of skill and chance
  • Taxation issues under GST laws
  • Consumer protection concerns

Recommendation:

The paper proposes a centralized regulatory authority to streamline governance and promote industry growth.

AI Liability and Accountability: Who is Responsible?

This research focuses on liability issues arising from AI-driven decisions. It evaluates scenarios where AI systems cause harm and examines who should be held accountable—the developer, user, or system itself.

Key Questions:

  • Can AI be held legally accountable?
  • Should liability rest with developers or operators?
  • How can regulatory frameworks address these challenges?

Conclusion:

The study advocates for a shared liability model supported by regulatory oversight and mandatory risk assessment protocols.
