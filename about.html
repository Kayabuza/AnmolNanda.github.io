Profile

I am a law graduate with a focused interest in the evolving intersection of law and technology. My academic and professional journey reflects a consistent effort to understand how legal frameworks adapt to technological innovation, particularly in areas such as Artificial Intelligence, Data Protection, Cybersecurity, and Intellectual Property Rights.

With a foundation in both business and law, I approach legal problems not only from a doctrinal perspective but also with an understanding of practical and commercial implications. This dual perspective enables me to analyze legal issues in a structured, solution-oriented manner.

Academic Background

I completed my LL.B. from Lloyd Law College, where I developed a strong foundation in legal principles, research methodologies, and practical application of law. Prior to that, I pursued a BBA, which provided me with an understanding of business structures, management, and strategic thinking.

This combination has been instrumental in shaping my approach toward technology law, where legal knowledge must align with business realities and innovation.

Professional Experience & Internships

Lex Mantra – IPR & Data Protection Intern

At Lex Mantra, I gained hands-on experience in Intellectual Property Rights and Data Protection law. My work involved conducting detailed trademark searches, assisting in drafting trademark oppositions, and preparing documentation related to patent filings. I also engaged in research on GDPR and Indian data protection frameworks, analyzing compliance requirements and identifying regulatory gaps.

This experience helped me understand how intellectual property and data protection laws operate in practical scenarios and strengthened my ability to translate legal concepts into actionable solutions.

Saket District Court – Judicial Intern

During my internship at Saket District Court, I observed court proceedings and developed an understanding of litigation strategy, procedural law, and courtroom dynamics. I assisted in preparing case notes, analyzing judgments, and conducting legal research on various matters including civil disputes and human rights issues.

This experience enhanced my ability to think critically, understand judicial reasoning, and appreciate the practical application of legal principles.

Supreme Court (AOR Office)

Working under an Advocate-on-Record at the Supreme Court provided exposure to high-level litigation. I assisted in drafting case briefs, reviewing legal documents, and analyzing judgments related to civil and tax law matters. I also observed how legal strategy is developed in complex cases.

This experience refined my research skills and gave me insight into the depth and precision required in legal practice at the highest level.

Government Internships (SCLSC, NCPCR, Delhi Commission for Women)

My internships with government bodies allowed me to engage with legal work from a policy and social justice perspective. I participated in legal drafting, attended hearings, and was involved in fieldwork related to rights-based issues.

These experiences helped me understand the broader impact of law on society and strengthened my communication, client interaction, and problem-solving abilities.
